# 👨‍👩‍👧 Parent & Teacher Guide

This guide explains the **learning design** behind Learning Land and gives you a
ready-made **30-minute daily routine** for a KG1 / 5-year-old.

## 🎯 Learning goals (mapped to KG1)

| Area | What she practices | Skill being built |
|------|--------------------|-------------------|
| **Numbers** | Count objects 1–10, compare bigger/smaller, find the next/missing number, add small groups | Number sense, one-to-one counting, order, early addition |
| **Alphabets** | Match a picture to its starting letter, recognize upper-case letters, find what comes next | Letter recognition, **phonics** (letter–sound link), alphabet order |
| **Fun Quiz** | Animal sounds, sort things into groups (fruit/animal/vehicle), colors, real-life situations | General knowledge, classification, common-sense reasoning |
| **Think & Code** | Continue a pattern, odd-one-out, biggest/smallest, "what happens first?", more vs fewer | **Computational thinking**: patterns, sequencing, sorting, comparison |

### How this is "coding basics"
Coding is logical thinking before it is typing. The four pillars of
computational thinking — **patterns, sequencing, decomposition, and logic** — are
all here at a 5-year-old level:
- *Patterns:* "🔴🔵🔴🔵 → what's next?" (the basis of loops)
- *Sequencing:* "What do you do FIRST?" (the basis of step-by-step instructions)
- *Sorting & comparison:* "Which has more?", "biggest/smallest" (the basis of conditions)
- *Odd-one-out:* spotting the rule that connects things (the basis of categories)

When she's ready (age 6–7), the natural next step is a visual coding app like
ScratchJr or Code.org. This site lays the thinking groundwork.

## ⏱️ A 30-minute daily routine

A 5-year-old's focus comes in short bursts, so split the 30 minutes into rounds
with a tiny break and lots of praise. Each round below is one 8-question game.

| Time | Activity | Tip |
|------|----------|-----|
| 0–5 min | ⭐ **Daily Play** (warm-up) | Today's special mixed set |
| 5–10 min | 🔢 **Numbers** | Count out loud together |
| 10–11 min | 🤸 **Wiggle break** | Stretch, jump 5 times |
| 11–16 min | 🔤 **Alphabets** | Say the letter sound, not just its name |
| 16–21 min | 🍎 **Fun Quiz** | Ask "why?" after each answer |
| 21–22 min | 🤸 **Wiggle break** | Drink some water |
| 22–28 min | 🧠 **Think & Code** | Let her explain the pattern in her words |
| 28–30 min | 🏆 **Celebrate** | Count today's ⭐ stars together |

You can of course just let her tap whatever she likes — every game is the same
length and ends with a trophy.

## 💡 Tips to get the most out of it
- **Sit with her the first few times.** Read along, cheer, and let *her* tap.
- **Talk it through.** "There are three apples… one, two, three!" builds far more
  than tapping alone.
- **Wrong answers are fine** — the game just says "try again" and lets her retry.
  Struggling a little is how learning sticks.
- **Keep it short.** If she's done after 15 minutes, stop. Tomorrow brings fresh
  puzzles.
- **Celebrate the stars**, not just correct answers — effort over perfection.

## 🔊 About the voice
Every question is read aloud so a pre-reader can play independently. If you don't
hear it:
- Make sure **Sound: On** (toggle on the home screen).
- Use the **live web link** (some browsers mute speech on local files).
- Tap the **🔊** button on any question to replay it.

## ✏️ Customizing content
All the pictures and words live in `js/content.js`. You can add her favorite
animals, fruits, or new "what should you do?" situations and the games will use
them automatically — a nice way to keep it personal as she grows.
